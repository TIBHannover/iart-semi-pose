Motivation

Pose estimation in art:

  • Poses and gestures are important for iconographic concepts
  • Extraction of pose information allows for the analysis of large art datasets

Pose estimation in art data is very challenging:

  • No or very small collection of training images from the art domain
  • More abstract and stylistically diverse representations of poses than in real-world datasets
  • Finetuning and style transfer can reduce the domain gap, but do not close it

Goal

  • Building a state-of-the-art pose estimator for art-historical images
  • Utilization of semi-superviced techniques to make use of unlabeled art data
  • Comparison with other approaches based on style transfer or on relevant non-art data

Our Approach: Two-Stage Person Detector

Two-stage approach is based on two Transformer models [1]
  • First stage: prediction of bounding boxes for human appearances
  • Second stage: prediction of keypoints of cropped bounding boxes

Our Approach for Semi-supervised Pose Estimation

  • Adaptation of Soft Teacher [2] or FixMatch [3] approach
  • Use of labeled and unlabeled data for bounding box and keypoint prediction
      • Labeled data:
    • Student uses input image and ground truth label for optimization
      • Unlabeled data:
    • Teacher model predicts boxes or keypoints in a weakly augmented image
    • Student uses the strongly augmented image and the teacher's prediction for optimization

Datasets

  • Real-world data: Common detection and pose dataset COCO 2017
  • Style-transfered data: Use style transfer [5] on COCO 2017 images to create art versions
  • Labeled art data: People-Art and PoPArt contain manually annotated art images
  • Unlabeled art data: ART500k large dataset of art pictures

Conclusions and Future Work

Conclusion

  • We have evaluated various techniques for pose prediction in art
  • We have shown how a semi-supervised approach can be applied to pose estimation
  • Our semi-supervised PoPArt model outperforms the other baseline models

Outlook

  • Our method could be applied to more complex Transformer models even with higher resolution
  • Evaluation of how semi-supervised learning methods can be combined with style transfer
  • Extend the PoPArt dataset with more bounding boxes
